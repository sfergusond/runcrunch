Intensity Score

Intensity score is the difference between your current effort and a function describing your theoretical best efforts at any distance given a recent best time and distance.

For example, if your best 5k time is 15:00 and you went for a 5km run in 20:00, your intensity would be 63%. A 10 mile run in 1:25:00 would result in an intensity score of 41%, and so on.

A run's overall intensity score is calculated with GAP data if available. For intensity scores dislayed in auto-lap and device-lap data, the intensity score is calculated for the distance of that lap. This is done to display more accurate info about interval training.

To prevent paces at walking pace or below to skew the result, the percentage range is scaled between your PR information and 15:00 min/mile pace or 2.25x your best theoretical mile PR, whichever is faster.

Zones are a percentage of your theoretical best/fastest pace at an arbitrary distance.
